### Physics

#### Research
I did my undergrad at MIT from 2014 - 2018, and my PhD at Berkeley from 2018 - December 2024.
My primary research interests are in thermal field theory, non-perturbative physics,
and dark matter + baryogenesis.

In the latter part of my PhD, I became focused on developing mathematical methods
for non-perturbatively calculating false-vacuum decay rates in the early universe,
which is relevant for a wide range of strongly-interacting field theories. The most
complete version of this question appears in the final chapter of my graduate dissertation,
[Phase transitions in the early universe](https://escholarship.org/uc/item/1205c73q).

#### Teaching
Teaching has always been one of my favourite parts of physics. I first started
teaching in high school, when I set up a program for older students to tutor
younger students in math.

At MIT, I had the chance to be an undergrad TA for 8.13: Junior Lab (aka JLab), the
awesome undergrad experimental physics lab class.

At Berkeley, I taught a number of courses, ranging from intro mechanics and E+M
to the advanced experimental lab and analytic mechanics.

#### Outreach + mentorship
At MIT, I had the chance to be part of the Physics Department working group
for several years, during which we worked to improve aspects of the department
culture. One of the results of that time was the department's Code of Conduct.

At Berkeley, I was part of IGenSpectrum (Identity and Gender Spectrum, pronounced
eigenspectrum), which aimed to foster community among queer members of the department.
